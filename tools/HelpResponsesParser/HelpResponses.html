What TWD
TWD stands for Trench Wars Divisions. It is an automated, bot-run league consisting of Warbird dueling (TWDD), Javelin dueling (TWDJ),Spider dueling (TWSD),Fighter dueling (TWFD) and Basing (TWDB). More information can be found at http://twd.trenchwars.org/documents/introduction .

What TWDD
TWDD stands for Trench Wars Dueling Division. It is a pure warbird deathmatch between 2 different squads; either 4v4 or 5v5 to 10 deaths per squad member. The matches are held in ?go twdd arenas.

What TWJD
TWJD stands for Trench Wars Javelin Division. It is a pure javelin deathmatch between 2 different squads; either 4v4 or 5v5 to 10 deaths per squad member. The matches are held in ?go twjd arenas.

What TWBD
TWBD stands for Trench Wars Basing Division. It is a basing style timed race between 2 different squads (much like Pure Public arenas). First squad to hold flag for 15 minutes wins. Games can range between 5-8 players per team. Common ships used are the terrier, spiders and sharks. Warbirds, Javelins and Lancaster are rarely used. The matches are held in ?go twbd arenas.

What TWSD
TWSD stands for Trench Wars Spider Division. It is a pure spider deathmatch between 2 different squads; either 4v4 or 5v5 up to a combined of 50 deaths per squad. The matches are held in ?go twsd arenas.

What TWFD
TWFD stands for Trench Wars Fighter Division.It is a deathmatch of fighter ships(spiders,warbirds,lancasters) between 2 different squads; either 4v4 or 5v5 to 10 deaths per squad member.The matches are held in ?go twfd arenas.

Repel (What )
When you use a repel, all nearby enemy bullets, bombs and ships will be pushed away from you. The icon on the right side of your screen depicting a yellow circle indicates the number of repels that your ship currently has. In public arenas, only the Shark (Ship 8) can equip repels.

Repel (How ) [Buy ]
To use a repel press <Shift> and <Ctrl> together. In public arenas, only the Shark (Ship 8) can equip and use repels.

Portal (What )
When you "drop" a portal, a marker will be placed on the map. The next time that you warp, you will be transported to the marker as opposed to the spawn area. Portals expire after a certain amount of time as indicated in the top right hand corner of the screen and you may only have one active portal at a given time. The icon on the right side of your screen depicting a target indicates the number of portals that your ship currently has. In public arenas, only the Leviathan (Ship 4), and the Terrier (Ship 5) can equip portals.

Portal (How ) [Buy ]
To use a portal, press <Shift> and <Ins> at the same time. In public arenas, only the Leviathan (Ship 4), and the Terrier (Ship 5) can equip and use portals.

In public arenas, only the Javelin (Ship 2), the Leviathan (Ship 4), and the Weasel (Ship 6) can equip rockets.

A log is a recorded transcript of your chat that is saved to a file.

Log (How )
To toggle the logging of your chat on and off, type ?log Filename where <Filename> is the name of the log file to be created. If no Filename is specified then it will be saved in Session.log.

What TWL
TWL stands for Trench Wars Leagues. It has three divisions: Javelin (TWLJ), Dueling (TWLD), and Basing (TWLB). Javelin and Dueling play matches with 5 players per team, while basing features 8 players per team. Months before the league starts, squads compete in duels for ranking. In order to qualify for TWL, a squad must play 15 games in one division. The exact number of squads that are invited to participate depends on the amount of competition and demand in a specific division. If a squad drops out of the league, then the next highest ranking squad is invited to play. The TWL season is a very competitive time for most squads. It brings the most skilled players to the same arena in order to battle it out for victory and gain the esteem from all players as "the best." It is the highlight of Trench Wars and its reputation and prestige can not be compared to any other zone in Continuum.
